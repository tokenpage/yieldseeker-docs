# FAQ

## What assets does Yield Seeker currently support?

Yield Seeker currently supports **USDC** on the **Base** network.

Support for additional assets, including **cbBTC** and **wETH**, has already been integrated into the protocol architecture and will become available as additional vaults are enabled.

---

## How does the allocation engine make decisions?

Every allocation follows the same structured decision pipeline.

Candidate vaults are first evaluated against eligibility requirements before being scored based on yield, liquidity, protocol characteristics, and portfolio constraints.

Only after these stages are complete does the execution framework perform any on-chain transactions.

For more information, see **Decision Pipeline**.

---

## Does Yield Seeker simply choose the highest APY?

No.

The allocation engine optimises for long-term, risk-adjusted returns rather than the highest advertised APY.

Factors such as liquidity, transaction costs, collateral quality, diversification, and user preferences all influence allocation decisions.

---

## Can I tell my agent to avoid certain protocols?

Yes.

Agents support natural-language rules that allow you to customise portfolio behaviour.

Examples include:

- Never use a particular vault.
- Only allocate to specific protocols.
- Always maintain a minimum allocation in one protocol.
- Prefer stability over maximum yield.

These preferences become persistent rules for that individual agent.

---

## Can I see why my agent made a decision?

Yes.

Every agent includes a **Discuss** tab where you can ask questions about current allocations, previous decisions, active rules, or portfolio changes.

The agent explains its reasoning in natural language.

---

## How often does my agent rebalance?

Agents continuously evaluate supported opportunities at regular intervals.

Rebalancing only occurs when the expected improvement justifies the associated transaction costs and remains consistent with your configured risk profile.

---

## Can the agent move funds anywhere on-chain?

No.

Agents cannot execute arbitrary blockchain transactions.

Every action must pass through registered adapters, interact only with approved protocol targets, and satisfy protocol-specific validation before execution is permitted.

For more information, see **Execution Model**.

---

## Does Yield Seeker have access to my funds?

No.

Only the owner of an Agent Wallet can initiate withdrawals.

Yield Seeker cannot withdraw assets from your wallet or transfer them to arbitrary destinations.

---

## What happens if Yield Seeker goes offline?

Your assets remain inside your Agent Wallet.

If the frontend is unavailable, our team can assist you in interacting directly with the underlying smart contracts to recover your assets.

The application provides convenience—not custody.

---

## What happens if one of the supported protocols is exploited?

Yield Seeker continuously monitors supported protocols.

If elevated risk is detected, the allocation engine may attempt to reduce or remove exposure where possible.

However, as with any DeFi protocol, no system can guarantee immediate exits during extreme market-wide liquidity events.

---

## Can I withdraw my funds at any time?

Yes.

Users can withdraw their assets whenever they choose.

Withdrawals are initiated by the wallet owner and do not require approval from Yield Seeker.

---

## How are new protocols added?

Yield Seeker supports new protocols through modular adapters.

Before becoming available, new integrations must be:

- reviewed
- registered on-chain
- approved through hardware-backed multisignature controls
- protected by a four-day administrative timelock

This ensures protocol extensions remain transparent and predictable.

---

## Has Yield Seeker been audited?

Yes.

Yield Seeker's smart contracts have been independently audited by **Nethermind**.

The full audit report and source code are publicly available from the project's GitHub repository.
