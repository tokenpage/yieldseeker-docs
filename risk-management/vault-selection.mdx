# Vault Selection

Yield Seeker evaluates every supported vault using a combination of objective eligibility requirements, risk controls, and user-defined preferences.

Rather than relying on a single risk score, multiple independent signals are considered before capital is allocated.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

Before a vault becomes eligible, it must satisfy minimum operational requirements.

These include:

- minimum total value locked (TVL)
- sufficient withdrawable liquidity
- supported vault type
- acceptable collateral composition
- protocol availability

Vaults that fail these requirements are excluded before scoring begins.

---

## Liquidity

Yield generation is only useful if positions can be exited efficiently.

Yield Seeker evaluates available withdrawable liquidity and limits allocations where necessary to reduce the likelihood of becoming a disproportionate participant within any individual vault.

Rather than rejecting otherwise suitable opportunities, allocations may be reduced to match available liquidity.

---

## Diversification

Concentrating an entire portfolio inside a single protocol introduces unnecessary risk.

The allocation engine therefore applies portfolio constraints that limit exposure to individual vaults, helping diversify capital across multiple opportunities where appropriate.

---

## Supported Protocol Types

Not every DeFi strategy is treated equally.

By default, Yield Seeker prioritises established overcollateralised lending markets.

More advanced strategy types, such as aggregator vaults, can be enabled through different risk profiles where appropriate.

---

## Collateral Quality

Yield Seeker evaluates the assets underlying each supported vault.

Certain collateral assets may be excluded entirely, while additional on-chain liquidity analysis helps identify assets with limited market depth or trading activity.

This helps reduce exposure to illiquid or higher-risk collateral.

---

## User Rules

Users are able to customise how their own agent allocates capital.

Examples include:

- Never use a particular vault.
- Only allocate to specific protocols.
- Always maintain a minimum allocation to one protocol.
- Prefer stable returns over short-term APY spikes.
- Limit maximum exposure to an individual vault.

These preferences become persistent rules for that individual agent and are automatically incorporated into future allocation decisions.

---

## Conservative and Explorative Profiles

Every agent operates using a configurable risk profile.

The default **Conservative** profile prioritises larger, more established opportunities with stricter allocation constraints.

The **Explorative** profile broadens the range of eligible opportunities while maintaining the same execution safeguards.

Users can switch between profiles or create more specific rules through natural language conversations with their agent.

---

## Curated Protocol Support

Yield Seeker currently maintains a curated set of supported protocols and vaults.

New integrations undergo technical review before being registered within the protocol and are only made available after completing the administrative approval process and four-day timelock.

This ensures that execution remains restricted to protocols that have been explicitly reviewed and approved.
